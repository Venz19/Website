✙ 1939 ✙





- The Outbreak of World War II -




-- -- -- o O o -- -- --

The Second World War began at the 1st of September 1939, when Hitler launched his invasion of Poland. The Poles fought bravely, but they were heavily outnumbered in both men and machines, espcially to air forces. Britain and France declared war on Germany on the 3rd of September 1939, just a couple days after the start of war, but gave no real assistance to Poland. Two weeks later, Stalin then invaded eastern Poland which forced its army to face two fronts, until on the 27th of September, Warsaw surrendered. Organized Polish resistance ceased after another week’s fighting. Poland was later on divided into two parts, Germany had the West while Russia had the East.

✙

The Nazis unleashed a reign of terror that was eventually to claim six million victims, half of whom were Polish Jews murdered in extermination camps. The Soviet regime was no less harsh. Poland’s then-leader Marshal Eduard Smigly-Rydz had warned, “With the Germans we risk losing our liberty, but with the Russians we lose our soul.”
